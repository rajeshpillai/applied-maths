00 · Introduction

Tier 4 — Pre-Calculus and Calculus · Grade 12 · The two great ideas of calculus — derivatives and integrals — and the algebra of vectors that lifts geometry into 3D

What this tier is for

For three centuries the great unsolved problem of mathematics was describing change — the speed of a falling stone, the area under a curve, the slope of a tangent line. Newton and Leibniz solved it in the 1660s by inventing calculus: a single framework that tames limits, derivatives, and integrals.

By the end of Tier 4 you can:

  • Compute limits and recognise continuity.
  • Compute derivatives of polynomials, trig, exp, and log functions; apply the chain rule.
  • Use derivatives for optimisation, related rates, and linear approximation.
  • Compute definite integrals as areas; apply the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
  • Solve simple differential equations.
  • Work with vectors in 2D and 3D, including dot and cross products.
  • Describe motion with parametric equations.
