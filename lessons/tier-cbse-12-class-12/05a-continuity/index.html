Ch 5a — Continuity

Draw the curve without lifting your pen — and what that really means

Pre-flight check

  1. Can you compute simple limits like \lim_{x \to 2}(x^2 + 1) = 5?
  2. Do you know \lim_{x \to 0} \dfrac{\sin x}{x} = 1?
  3. Comfortable evaluating f(x) at a point and “from both sides”?

If no on (1) or (2), revisit Class 11 · Limits.

Touch

Three sketches. Imagine each as the path of your pen.

Curve A: f(x) = x^2. A smooth U. Pen never lifts.

Curve B: g(x) = \begin{cases} x & x < 1 \\ x + 2 & x \ge 1 \end{cases}. Two line segments. At x = 1 the curve jumps from 1 to 3. You must lift your pen.

Curve C: h(x) = \begin{cases} \sin x / x & x \ne 0 \\ 0 & x = 0 \end{cases}. The graph approaches 1 as x \to 0, but the dot plotted at x = 0 is at 0. Removable hole.

Continuity is exactly the question: can you draw it through that point without lifting? A continuous function at a satisfies all three of the checks below.

Three things must agree at the test point a:

  1. f(a) is defined.
  2. \lim_{x \to a} f(x) exists.
  3. The two equal each other.

Curve A passes everywhere. Curve B fails at x = 1 (jump). Curve C fails at x = 0 (defined value disagrees with the limit).

Wonder

  1. Could a function be defined at a and have a limit at a but still not be continuous there?
  2. Is “continuous on the whole domain” automatic for polynomials?
  3. What sort of physical signal is most visibly discontinuous — temperature, sound, voltage at a switch?

Discover

Problem 1

Show f(x) = x^2 is continuous at x = 3.

Hint 1 — a nudge Compute f(3) and \lim_{x \to 3} f(x) separately.
Full solution f(3) = 9. \lim_{x \to 3} x^2 = 9 (polynomial limits are evaluation). Equal. ✓ Continuous.

Problem 2

Test continuity of g(x) = \begin{cases} 2x + 1 & x < 1 \\ 4 & x \ge 1 \end{cases} at x = 1.

Hint 1 — a nudge Left limit, right limit, g(1).
Full solution LHS limit = 2(1) + 1 = 3. RHS limit = 4. They disagree — limit doesn’t exist. Discontinuous at x = 1.

Problem 3

For what value of k is h(x) = \begin{cases} kx + 2 & x \le 2 \\ x^2 - 1 & x > 2 \end{cases} continuous at x = 2?

Hint 1 — a nudge Match left and right limits at x = 2.
Full solution LHS at 2: 2k + 2. RHS at 2: 4 - 1 = 3. So 2k + 2 = 3 \Rightarrow k = 1/2. Also h(2) = 2k + 2 = 3. ✓

Problem 4

Test f(x) = \dfrac{\sin x}{x} for x \ne 0, f(0) = 1, at x = 0.

Hint 1 — a nudge Use the standard limit \lim_{x \to 0} \sin x / x = 1.
Full solution \lim_{x \to 0} f(x) = 1 = f(0). ✓ Continuous (with the gap filled).

Problem 5

Identify the type of discontinuity of f(x) = \dfrac{1}{x - 1} at x = 1.

Full solution f(1) is undefined. As x \to 1^-, f \to -\infty. As x \to 1^+, f \to +\infty. Infinite discontinuity (vertical asymptote).

Bolzano’s 1817 manifesto for rigour. Bernard Bolzano, a Bohemian priest-mathematician working in obscurity, published Rein analytischer Beweis — “Purely Analytic Proof” — in 1817, fifty-five years before Weierstrass made the epsilon-delta definition standard. He gave the first rigorous proof of the Intermediate Value Theorem and, in the same paper, the first modern definition of a continuous function: for every \varepsilon > 0 there exists \delta > 0 such that |x - a| < \delta implies |f(x) - f(a)| < \varepsilon. Almost no one read it. The paper sat unnoticed in a Prague journal until Weierstrass independently rediscovered the same idea in the 1860s. The “draw without lifting your pen” picture you used in Touch is exactly the intuition Bolzano insisted needed an algebraic test.

Continuity is the marriage of three things at one point. A continuous function at x = a needs f(a) to exist, \lim_{x \to a} f(x) to exist, and the two to match. Drop any one — undefined value, mismatched one-sided limits, or a removable discontinuity where the dot is misplaced — and the curve breaks. Every classification in this lesson is a different way one of those three checks can fail.

Name

A function f is continuous at x = a iff \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a). Equivalently, three things hold: f(a) exists, \lim_{x\to a}f(x) exists, and they match.

f is continuous on a set S iff it is continuous at every point of S.

Algebra of continuous functions. If f, g are continuous at a, so are f + g, f - g, fg, f/g (provided g(a) \ne 0), and f \circ g (composite).

Standard continuous functions. Polynomials, \sin, \cos, e^x, \log x (on their natural domains) are continuous everywhere on those domains.

A verse:

Three checks at a — value defined, limit alive, the two agree; Polynomials, sine, exp — all continuous, all the way. Jump means LHS, RHS disagree; Hole means limit lives but the point is misplaced.

Apply

Worked example

Find a, b if f(x) = \begin{cases} ax + 1 & x \le 3 \\ bx + 3 & x > 3 \end{cases} is continuous everywhere and f(2) = 5.

A competent solver:

  1. f(2) = 2a + 1 = 5 \Rightarrow a = 2.
  2. Continuity at x = 3: 3a + 1 = 3b + 3 \Rightarrow 7 = 3b + 3 \Rightarrow b = 4/3.

Linear pieces are continuous on each piece; only the join needs care.

Practice — Warm

W1. Show f(x) = |x| is continuous at x = 0.

Full solution f(0) = 0. LHS limit = \lim_{x\to0^-}(-x) = 0. RHS limit = 0. All agree. ✓

W2. For what value of c is f(x) = \begin{cases} cx^2 & x < 1 \\ 4x - 1 & x \ge 1 \end{cases} continuous at x = 1?

Full solution c(1)^2 = 4(1) - 1 \Rightarrow c = 3.

W3. Is f(x) = \tan x continuous at x = \pi/2?

Full solution \tan(\pi/2) is undefined; in fact \tan x \to \pm\infty as x \to \pi/2. Not continuous at \pi/2 (infinite discontinuity).

Practice — Working

P1. Test continuity of f(x) = \begin{cases} \dfrac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2} & x \ne 2 \\ 4 & x = 2 \end{cases} at x = 2.

Hint 1 — a nudge Factor the numerator.
Full solution For x \ne 2: \frac{(x-2)(x+2)}{x-2} = x + 2. Limit as x \to 2 is 4. f(2) = 4. ✓ Continuous.

P2. Find a such that f(x) = \begin{cases} \dfrac{1 - \cos x}{x^2} & x \ne 0 \\ a & x = 0 \end{cases} is continuous at 0.

Hint 1 — a nudge 1 - \cos x = 2 \sin^2(x/2).
Full solution \lim_{x\to0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x^2} = \lim \frac{2\sin^2(x/2)}{x^2} = \frac{1}{2}\lim \left(\frac{\sin(x/2)}{x/2}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{2}. So a = 1/2.

P3. Show f(x) = \begin{cases} x \sin(1/x) & x \ne 0 \\ 0 & x = 0 \end{cases} is continuous at 0.

Hint 1 — a nudge |\sin(1/x)| \le 1, so |x \sin(1/x)| \le |x|.
Full solution By squeeze: -|x| \le x\sin(1/x) \le |x|. Both bounds \to 0 as x\to0. So \lim = 0 = f(0). ✓

Practice — Stretch

S1. Discuss continuity of f(x) = \lfloor x \rfloor (greatest integer function) on \mathbb{R}.

Full solution At any non-integer a, f is constant on a small neighbourhood — continuous. At an integer n: LHS limit = n - 1 (from below) but f(n) = n — jump. Discontinuous at every integer; continuous elsewhere.

Board-Pattern Practice

1-mark MCQ #1. f(x) = \frac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3} for x \ne 3, f(3) = 6. At x = 3, f is (a) continuous (b) jump (c) infinite (d) removed.

Full solution \lim = 6 = f(3). (a) continuous.

1-mark MCQ #2. Number of points where f(x) = |x| + |x - 1| is not differentiable on \mathbb{R} is (a) 0 (b) 1 (c) 2 (d) 3. (Continuity question implied — at corners.)

Full solution Corners at x = 0, x = 1. Continuous everywhere (sum of continuous); not differentiable at 2 points. (c) 2. (Note: continuity holds everywhere.)

2-mark. Examine continuity of f(x) = \begin{cases} x + 5 & x \le 1 \\ x - 5 & x > 1 \end{cases} at x = 1.

Full solution LHS = 6, RHS = -4. Disagree. Discontinuous (jump).

2-mark. Find k if f(x) = \begin{cases} kx + 1 & x \le 5 \\ 3x - 5 & x > 5 \end{cases} is continuous at x = 5.

Full solution 5k + 1 = 10 \Rightarrow k = 9/5.

3-mark. Find a, b such that f(x) = \begin{cases} 5 & x \le 2 \\ ax + b & 2 < x < 10 \\ 21 & x \ge 10 \end{cases} is continuous on \mathbb{R}.

Full solution At x = 2: 2a + b = 5. At x = 10: 10a + b = 21. Subtract: 8a = 16 \Rightarrow a = 2, b = 1.

3-mark. Test continuity of f(x) = \begin{cases} \dfrac{\sin 3x}{x} & x \ne 0 \\ k & x = 0 \end{cases} at x = 0. Find k so f is continuous.

Full solution \lim_{x\to0} \sin 3x / x = 3 \lim \sin 3x / (3x) = 3. So k = 3.

5-mark long answer. Discuss the continuity of f(x) = \begin{cases} \dfrac{\sqrt{1 + kx} - \sqrt{1 - kx}}{x} & -1 \le x < 0 \\ \dfrac{2x + 1}{x - 2} & 0 \le x \le 1 \end{cases} at x = 0. Find k.

Hint 1 — a nudge Rationalise the LHS expression. Compute LHS limit and f(0).
Full solution LHS: rationalise. \frac{\sqrt{1+kx} - \sqrt{1-kx}}{x} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{1+kx} + \sqrt{1-kx}}{\sqrt{1+kx} + \sqrt{1-kx}} = \frac{(1+kx) - (1-kx)}{x(\sqrt{1+kx} + \sqrt{1-kx})} = \frac{2k}{ \sqrt{1+kx} + \sqrt{1-kx}}. As x \to 0^-: = \frac{2k}{2} = k. RHS at 0: f(0) = (0+1)/(0-2) = -1/2. For continuity, k = -1/2.

6-mark Case Study. Tax slabs as a piecewise function.

A simplified income-tax model: T(x) = \begin{cases} 0 & x \le 250{,}000 \\ 0.05(x - 250{,}000) & 250{,}000 < x \le 500{,}000 \\ 12{,}500 + 0.20(x - 500{,}000) & 500{,}000 < x \le 1{,}000{,}000 \\ 112{,}500 + 0.30(x - 1{,}000{,}000) & x > 1{,}000{,}000 \end{cases}

  1. Is T continuous at x = 250{,}000? At x = 500{,}000? Justify. (3 marks)
  2. Is T continuous at x = 1{,}000{,}000? (1 mark)
  3. Why is continuity desirable for tax law? (2 marks)
Full solution (i) At 250{,}000: LHS = 0, RHS = 0. ✓ At 500{,}000: LHS = 0.05 \cdot 250{,}000 = 12{,}500. RHS = 12{,}500. ✓ (ii) At 1{,}000{,}000: LHS = 12{,}500 + 0.20 \cdot 500{,}000 = 112{,}500. RHS = 112{,}500. ✓ (iii) A jump at a slab boundary would mean someone earning ₹1 extra pays substantially more tax — incentivising reporting tricks just below the boundary. Continuity ensures fairness in marginal behaviour.

NCERT Exemplar pointer: Ex 5.1 Q1–14. Previous-year pointer: CBSE 2023 Set 1 Q23 (3-mark); CBSE 2022 Q15.

Common confusions

  1. “Continuous means smooth.” No. |x| is continuous everywhere but not smooth at 0 — a corner. Smoothness is differentiability; continuity is weaker.
  2. “If f(a) is defined, f is continuous at a.” Defined-at-a is one of three requirements; the limit must also exist and equal f(a).
  3. “Discontinuity means f is undefined.” Not necessarily. f can be defined at a but still discontinuous (Curve B, Curve C above).

Takeaways

If you remember nothing else from this lesson, remember these:

  1. Three checks at the test point. Value defined, limit exists, the two agree — miss any one and the curve breaks.
  2. Polynomials, \sin, \cos, e^x, \log x are continuous on their natural domains. That’s a free pass; you only sweat at piecewise joins, denominators, and \log at non-positive numbers.
  3. Three flavours of discontinuity. Jump (one-sided limits disagree), removable (limit exists but the dot is misplaced), infinite (vertical asymptote).

Where this matters

AI/ML. ReLU activation is continuous but has a corner at 0; sigmoid is smooth. Network designers care about which discontinuities the optimiser tolerates.

Research / engineering. Physical laws are usually modelled by continuous functions; jumps signal a phase transition or impact event. Numerical integrators must detect and handle these.
